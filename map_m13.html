Milestone 13

Fire History in Northern New Mexico

Closeness of fire occurrence to human settlements and critical infrastructure

Wildfires in the United Sates' southwest region are a common phenomena that, in the past couple of decades,
have made headlines for their impacts on human settlements/developments, as well as effects on the natural
environment. Post-fire flash flood and mass movement events, prompted by the creation of impervious soils
by the fires, bring a new load fo materials (soil, rocks, woody debris) down stream, which can pose a threat
to buildings and transportation infrastructure.

The goal of the map below is to help visualize the closeness and intersection of fire boundaries to
populated areas, including Native Ameican Reservations, a well as roads and bridges in northern New Mexico.
Th map contains a set of layers obtained from the New Mexico Resource Geographic Information System (RGIS),
and include the following: (1) fire history in the form of fire perimeters, (2) Native-American reservation areas,
(3) populated areas and (4) bridges that intersect with fire permeters, and (5) New Mexico counties.

It is important to know that, due to the lack of information about the year bridges/culverts were constructed, the
the bridge/culvert subset does not consider wether they were there when the fire(s) occurred. This means that
some bridges/culverts may have been constructed after the overlapping fire occurred. Thus, the bridges/culverts
included in the map represent ones that were constructed in areas burned bu fires, that were (potentially) affected
by fire heat, or might have been closed due to closeness to fire, affecting transit in the area.

You can zoom out to view the data presented for all of New Mexico.
