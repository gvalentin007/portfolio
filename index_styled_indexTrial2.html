About me

My name is Gladys Valentin. I am a on my second year as a graduate student at the Department of Geography and Environmental Studies, UNM, majoring in GIScience. My masters thesis deals with assessing vegetation response to fire frequency and fire severity in Bandelier National Monument through Remote Sensing and GIS.

Why I'm taking the course

While doing research for my thesis, I stumbled upon a website created by a graduate student, where she shared content related to her masters thesis. I was inspired to learn more about creating a personal website/online portfolio where I could share my academic work.

Topics I'm interested in

  • Natural and anthropogenic hazards
  • Remote Sensing
  • Geographic Information Science
  • Human-environment interactions
  • Watershed dynamics
  • Island environments
  • Beach morphology
  • Photography

Types of data I have worked with (themes & formats)

  • Geospatial vector data including shapefiles (.shp), .gml and .kml within ArcGIS, QGIS, and Google Earth
  • Raster data such as georeferenced satellite imagery and aerial photography (GEOTIFF, .img)
  • Raster data generated in remote sensing software including TerrSet and ERDAS Imagine
  • Tabular data in .xls, .xlxs, .csv and .dbf formats
  • XML metadata files
  • Adobe Illustrator files (.ai)
  • Photographs, images, and exported maps in .img, .png, .jpg, and .emf formats
  • Text file in .docx, .txt, and .pdf formats
