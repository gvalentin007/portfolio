Sub-aerial Beach Inventory of Puerto Rico

Sub-aerial beaches are defined as deposits of unconsolidated sediments, including sand or gravel, extending from permanent physiographic features
landward (vegetation, dunes and/or man-made structures) to the high water level. This map presents a sub-aerial beach inventory
I created during my undergraduate studies at the University of Puerto Rico, using ArcGIS 10.1 through interpretation of high-resulotuion imagery collected in 2010.
Characteristics such as beach area, length, presence of beachrock and other barriers, river mouths, and adjacent structures were added to the attribute table.
A total of 1,225 sub-aerial beaches were identified in Puerto Rico mainland, Vieques and Culebra island-municipalities,
and Caja de Muertos Island. This beach inventory represents the first detailed geographic database of beaches in Puerto Rico, which can be updated and
could serve to monitor morphological changes and data relevant to coastal studies.

Zoom in to any of the arrow markers of beast beaches and you will see golden-colored polygons representing the boundaries of sub-aerial beaches.
These can be found throughout the coast of Puerto Rico and municipality-islands. Click the markers and polygons to find out more about each of them.

Note that the digitization of beach structures was based on the interpretation of imagery that is not the one displayed in this map.
Thus, the beach polygons may not coincide with the imagery or basemaps provided in the map, and may appear to be misplaced. That said, you will be able
to have a glimpse of how dynamic the erosion and deposition of sediments can be. You will see that in most places the landward boubdary matches
the vegetation/man-made structure lines, but not the high-water line. This is because sand has been removed or added through natural processes.
This change occurs all throughout the year. But some beaches are suffering erosion without recovery, especially in areas with adjacent human develompent.

I chose to display this shapefile for our Deep Dive 1 to learn how to integrate spatial data using Google Fusion Tables
on Google API maps. In this map I used a hybrid basemap, so that the audience can visualize the beach systems through the satellite image,
and also be able to see the road network and places of interest near them. This map also includes the markers created for Milestone 4,
as well as a few additional beach locations, to show some of the best beaches in the Island vis a vis beach boundaries from the inventory.
