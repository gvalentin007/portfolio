About me:
My name is Gladys Valentin. I am a on my second year as graduate student at the Department of Geography and Environmental Studies, UNM, majoring in GIScience. My masters thesis deals with assessing vegetation response to the Las Conchas fire in Bandleier National Monument through Remote Sensing and GIS.

Why I'm taking the course to learn how to share geospatial content on the web. While doing research for my thesis, I stumbled upon a website created by a graduate student, where she shared content related to her masters thesis. I was inspired to learn more about creating a website to share my academic work.

Topics I'm interested in

  • Natural and anthrpogenic hazards
  • Remote Sensing
  • Geographic Information Science
  • Human-environment interactions
  • Watershed dynamics
  • Island environments
  • Beach morphology

Types of data I have worked with (themes & formats)

  • Geospatial vector data including shapefiles (.shp) within Esri's ArcGIS and QGIS, and .kml
  • Raster data such as georeferences satellite imagery and aerial photography (GEOTIFF, .img)
  • Raster data generated in remote sensing software including TerrSet and ERDAS Imagine.
  • Tabular data in .xls, .xlxs, and .dbf formats
  • XML metadata files
  • Adobe Illustrator files (.ai)
  • Photographs, images, and exported maps in .img, .png, .jpg, .emf formats
  • Text file in .docx, .txt, and .pdf formats
